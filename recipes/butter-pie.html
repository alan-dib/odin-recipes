Butter Flaky Pie Crust

How to make a delicious Buttery Flaky Crust Pie

This flaky pie crust recipe is perfect for beginner bakers and seasoned professionals
— it'll quickly become a staple in your recipe box.

What you will need:

  • Flour

    This flaky pie crust recipe starts with all-purpose flour.

  • Salt

    A pinch of salt enhances the flavor and promotes a tender, moist crust.
  • Butter

    Cold butter adds rich flavor and creates steam as it melts,
    ensuring a perfectly flaky finished product.
  • Water

    Cold water acts as a binding agent which holds the dry ingredients together.
    It also contributes to the flakiness of the crust as it evaporates.

What Makes a Pie Crust Flaky?

When it comes to making a flaky pie crust, it's all about the fat
(in this case, butter). The fat works in two ways:

  1. It coats the flour, preventing it from absorbing the liquid and creating gluten.
    Too much gluten produces a dense, chewy crust.
  2. As the fat melts during baking, it leaves air pockets in the dough.
    The pockets fill with steam and expand, creating layers of flaky pastry crust.

For the flakiest crust, make sure your ingredients are as cold as possible to prevent the fat from melting before
it makes it to the oven. It also helps to work with cold hands and, if possible, a marble rolling pin and cutting board.

How to Make a Flaky Pie Crust

If you're intimidated by making homemade pie dough, you've come to the right place.
You'll find the full, step-by-step recipe below —
but here's a brief overview of what you can expect when you make this pie crust:

  1. Combine the flour and salt.

  2. Cut in the cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Add the cold water a tablespoon at a time.

  4. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  5. Roll the dough into a circle, then press it into a pie plate. Trim the excess and flute the edges.
